Most Real Estate Websites Are Glorified Business Cards

Most agent websites are template-built business cards: a headshot, a bio, some MLS listings, and a contact form nobody fills out. They don't rank in Google. They don't capture leads. They don't generate business.

A real estate website should be a lead generation asset. Every page should capture a buyer lead, a seller lead, or rank for a local search term. If it's not doing at least one of those things, it's costing you money every day.

What Makes a Real Estate Website Actually Generate Leads

Most agents think the problem is their website design. It is not. The problem is that their website was never built to generate leads in the first place. Here are the four things that separate a lead-generating website from a digital business card.

Custom Design, Not Templates

KVCore, Sierra, Placester — they all look the same because they are the same. Custom design means every layout, form, and CTA is optimized for your market, not the average agent.

IDX Integration Done Right

Live MLS data on your site. Visitors search homes, save favorites, set alerts — and every interaction captures their contact info. We integrate iHomeFinder, Showcase IDX, and IDX Broker with clean design and fast performance.

Lead Capture on Every Page

Every page gets a contextual capture mechanism: neighborhood guides, showing requests, home valuations, content upgrades. No matter where a visitor lands, there is a clear path to becoming a lead.

Speed and Core Web Vitals

Most agent sites load in 4-8 seconds on mobile. Ours target under 2. Every additional second costs you 7% in conversions. Clean code, optimized images, fast hosting.

Why Most Agents Should Start With Lead Generation, Not a New Website

Here is something most web design companies will not tell you: if your primary goal right now is getting more leads and closing more deals, a new website is probably not the fastest path to that outcome.

A landing page generates leads faster than a full website.

A custom landing page takes 5 to 7 days to build and can start generating leads the moment ads go live. A full website takes 4 to 8 weeks. If you need leads this month, a website project is not the answer. A landing page paired with Google and Meta ads is.

We tell every prospective website client the same thing: if you are not already generating consistent leads from paid ads and landing pages, start there first. Build your pipeline, close some deals, and then invest in a full website that serves as the long-term hub of your brand and SEO strategy.
