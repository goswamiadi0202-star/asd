Four audiences. One city. You need four campaigns.

Tempe is the most audience-diverse market in the East Valley. Within 40 square miles, you have ASU parents searching "investment property near ASU," recent graduates searching "first home Tempe under $400k," families searching "South Tempe homes Kyrene schools," and downsizers searching "Tempe patio homes low maintenance." Segmentation is not optional in Tempe. It is the difference between profitable campaigns and wasted ad spend.

TEMPE MARKET CONTEXT

Understanding the Tempe real estate landscape.

The Tempe median home price sits around $500,000, but that single number obscures the market's true complexity. Waterfront homes in The Lakes command $700,000 to $900,000. Starter homes in South Tempe start in the low $400s. Student-area condos near ASU can be had for $250,000 to $350,000. Each price band represents a different buyer, a different marketing message, and a different conversion path.

ASU's influence on the Tempe market cannot be overstated. With over 70,000 students on the Tempe campus, the university creates permanent demand for rental properties, generates a pipeline of young professionals who become first-time buyers, and attracts out-of-state parents who purchase investment properties for their children's college years. Investor activity remains strong despite rising interest rates because rental demand near campus shows no signs of softening.

The family market in South Tempe remains inventory-constrained. Kyrene School District continues to drive demand, and homes in the Corona del Sol and Marcos de Niza attendance zones sell quickly when priced correctly. Agents who pair seller-focused campaigns with buyer campaigns in South Tempe capture both sides of the transaction in a tight-inventory environment.

The agents winning in Tempe understand that this is not one market -- it is four markets sharing a city name, and each one requires its own strategy.
