01 · BEST FOR SCOTTSDALE LUXURY AGENTS

ASD Digital — our pick for full-service

Location: Phoenix, AZPrice: $2,500–$10,000/moContract: Month-to-month

Full-service real estate marketing agency purpose-built for Scottsdale luxury agents. Unlike the others on this list, ASD Digital covers all three pillars — paid advertising, custom website, and brand identity — from one team. 02 · BEST FOR TEMPLATE-BASED LUXURY WEBSITES

Luxury Presence

Location: Los Angeles, CAPrice: $200–$1,000/mo + setupContract: Annual common

The most recognizable name in real estate websites. Luxury Presence serves thousands of agents with premium-feeling template websites that look custom at a distance. Their template library is legitimately beautiful and their production workflow is efficient. If you need a good-looking website launched quickly and don't want to commission something custom, they're the category leader.

Where they don't compete: they don't run your ads, they don't build your brand from positioning up (they execute on an existing brand), and the templates — while attractive — are used by many agents, so your site will inevitably resemble someone else's.

STRENGTHS

  • Category-leading template quality
  • Fast setup (days, not weeks)
  • Real estate-specific features built in
  • Premium visual aesthetic

LIMITATIONS

  • Websites only — no ads, no brand strategy
  • Template-based — you'll share a look with other agents
  • Annual commitments common
  • No Scottsdale-specific expertise
03 · BEST FOR DIY LEAD-GEN PLATFORM

Real Geeks

Location: NationalPrice: $299–$599/moContract: Monthly, cancel anytime

A template-based platform combining a website with a lead-generation marketplace. Popular with high-volume residential agents who want a turnkey tech stack at an affordable monthly price. You get a website, a CRM, and lead flow from their platform — but the leads are shared across the Real Geeks ecosystem and the templates are recognizable.

For Scottsdale luxury, Real Geeks is usually not a fit. The template aesthetic is residential-generic, not luxury, and shared leads don't convert well at $2M+ price points where buyers need a more considered presentation.

STRENGTHS

  • Affordable monthly price
  • Fast setup
  • Integrated CRM included
  • Month-to-month

LIMITATIONS

  • Shared leads (not exclusive)
  • Template design looks similar across agents
  • Not positioned for luxury markets
  • No brand or custom-website capabilities
04 · BEST FOR PREMIUM CUSTOM WEBSITES (NO ADS)

Agent Image

Location: Los Angeles, CAPrice: $3,500–$10,000+ build + hostingContract: One-time build

Long-established real estate website firm building fully custom sites for luxury agents. Quality of deliverable is legitimately high — their portfolio includes some of the most recognizable luxury agent websites in the country. If your only goal is a premium custom website and you have $5,000–$10,000 one-time budget, they deliver.

Missing from their offering: ads, brand strategy, and ongoing campaign management. You'll still need to hire an ad agency and a brand consultant separately, which reintroduces the three-vendor problem.

STRENGTHS

  • High-quality custom websites
  • 20+ year track record
  • Strong luxury portfolio
  • Own your site after build

LIMITATIONS

  • Websites only — no ads or brand
  • Significant one-time cost ($3,500+)
  • Long build timelines (8–12 weeks typical)
  • No Scottsdale specialization
05 · BEST FOR BUDGET-CONSCIOUS SOLO AGENTS

Placester

Location: NationalPrice: $99–$299/moContract: Monthly subscription

DIY website platform with IDX integration aimed at agents who want something up fast for minimal spend. It works for agents who genuinely don't need differentiation — if you're serving a residential market at standard price points and mostly getting leads from your sphere, Placester gives you a functional web presence.

For Scottsdale luxury, it's not the right tool. The template aesthetic is entry-level and doesn't communicate premium. No custom design, no brand strategy, no ad management.

STRENGTHS

  • Very affordable ($99/mo entry)
  • DIY, fast deployment
  • Basic IDX included

LIMITATIONS

  • Entry-level template design
  • Not positioned for luxury
  • No ads, no brand, no custom work
  • Heavy DIY time investment

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Picking a Scottsdale marketing agency.

There is no single 'best' — it depends on what you need. For a full-service agency handling marketing, websites, and brand from one team with Scottsdale-specific expertise, ASD Digital is purpose-built. For beautiful website templates at scale, Luxury Presence is the industry standard. For DIY platforms, Real Geeks and Placester are popular but template-limited. Agent Image is a legacy premium option. The right choice depends on whether you want a partner or a platform.
Evaluate on five criteria: (1) scope — do they cover marketing, websites, AND brand, or just one? (2) exclusivity — are leads shared with other agents? (3) local knowledge — do they know Scottsdale submarkets like Silverleaf, DC Ranch, Paradise Valley? (4) contract — month-to-month or locked in? (5) customization — templates or custom-built? The right answer for Scottsdale luxury agents usually weights all five equally.
Luxury Presence produces beautiful template-based websites that look premium but are not Scottsdale-specific and do not include ads or brand strategy. Real Geeks is a lead-gen platform with templated sites and shared lead flow — efficient but not differentiated. Both work well for specific needs but neither is a full-service agency. Scottsdale luxury agents who want a single partner often find them insufficient on their own.
Full-service Scottsdale marketing agencies typically charge $2,500 to $10,000 per month for solo agents through teams, with brokerage tiers $10,000+. DIY platforms like Real Geeks and Placester charge $99 to $499 per month but require you to do the marketing yourself. Luxury Presence websites start around $200 per month for templates with one-time setup fees. Agent Image is premium at $3,500 to $10,000+ one-time website cost.
Three tests. First, ask for examples of Scottsdale clients and their specific submarket coverage. Second, ask what happens if you cancel — if it is a long contract or you lose your website, that is a sign. Third, ask for their cost-per-lead on Scottsdale campaigns. Good agencies answer all three immediately; platforms and large brokered agencies often cannot answer the first two.
